# SYSTEM CONSTITUTION
**Platform:** Child First Platform (working title)
**Version:** 1.0 | **Date:** 2026-04-02
**Status:** Phase 0 — Governing Document

---

## ARTICLE I — MISSION AND PURPOSE

### 1.1 Mission Statement
To ensure that no child goes without food or basic necessities by building a transparent, accountable, institutionally-grade platform that connects willing donors with verified children in need — with every dollar traceable, every claim provable, and every child record protected.

### 1.2 System Purpose
This platform exists to:
- Receive and route donated funds to verified programs serving children lacking food and basic necessities
- Provide donors with real-time, verifiable evidence of how their contributions are used
- Protect the identity, dignity, and data of every beneficiary child
- Operate with institutional-grade controls so that the system earns and maintains trust across donors, regulators, auditors, and the public

### 1.3 What This System Is Not
- Not a financial instrument or investment vehicle
- Not a cryptocurrency token or speculative asset
- Not a social network or media platform
- Not a fee-generating commercial enterprise (operational costs are disclosed, not profit-driven)
- Not a replacement for direct government food programs — a supplementary, transparent bridge

---

## ARTICLE II — SCOPE AND BOUNDARIES

### 2.1 In Scope
- Children (under 18) lacking sufficient access to food, clean water, or defined basic necessities
- Donations from individuals, organizations, and institutions
- Campaign operators (verified nonprofits, schools, community organizations, verified families)
- Fund disbursement to verified programs and organizations
- Impact reporting and evidence collection
- Donor management and communication
- Legal compliance for fundraising in operating jurisdictions

### 2.2 Out of Scope (Phase 1)
- Direct delivery of food (platform facilitates funding, does not operate logistics)
- Medical or healthcare funding (separate category, separate legal requirements)
- Adult beneficiaries
- Political campaigns or advocacy funding
- Cryptocurrency-only donation flows (may be added in a later phase with additional controls)

### 2.3 Geographic Scope
- To be defined before launch based on legal entity jurisdiction and fundraising license coverage
- Must not solicit funds in jurisdictions where platform is not licensed to fundraise
- This is a launch blocker until resolved

## ARTICLE III — USERS, OPERATORS, AND ROLES

### 3.1 Donors
- Individuals or organizations contributing funds
- Have the right to see where their money went
- Have the right to a refund under defined conditions
- Have the right to know all fees before confirming a transaction
- Must be 18 or older to donate (minors may not donate without guardian)
- Are not investors and receive no financial return

### 3.2 Campaign Operators
- Verified organizations, schools, nonprofits, or community groups running a campaign
- Must complete verification before receiving funds
- Must submit disbursement documentation
- Must provide impact reports
- Are liable for accurate representation of beneficiaries and outcomes

### 3.3 Platform Administrators
- Internal operators with elevated access
- Cannot unilaterally withdraw donor funds
- All admin actions are logged and auditable
- Access is scoped — no single admin has all capabilities
- Admin access is reviewed quarterly

### 3.4 Beneficiaries (Children)
- The children receiving food or necessities funded through this platform
- Are not users of the platform (do not have accounts)
- Their data is held with the highest privacy protection
- Their identifiable information (names, photos, location below city level) is never public
- Their dignity is protected in all platform communications

### 3.5 Verifiers
- Independent parties who confirm that funds reached their stated destination
- May be third-party auditors, field officers, or partner organizations
- Verification events are recorded and publicly accessible in aggregate

### 3.6 Platform Owner / Founding Operator
- Legal entity responsible for platform operation
- Must be a registered legal entity before launch
- Identity is publicly disclosed
- Cannot benefit financially beyond documented operational cost recovery

## ARTICLE IV — TRUST ASSUMPTIONS

### 4.1 What the System Must Prove (not assume)
- That donated funds reach stated programs
- That beneficiaries are real and verified
- That campaign claims are accurate
- That platform fees are disclosed before transaction
- That child data is protected

### 4.2 What the System Cannot Guarantee
- That every campaign operator acts in good faith (mitigated by verification and controls)
- That third-party delivery organizations perform perfectly
- That impact reports from field operators are 100% accurate in every case
- That donated funds produce guaranteed nutritional outcomes

### 4.3 Explicit Trust Non-Assumptions
- The system does NOT assume donors understand crypto or blockchain
- The system does NOT assume campaign operators are technically sophisticated
- The system does NOT assume regulators will be lenient because the mission is good
- The system does NOT assume media coverage will be positive — it must withstand hostile scrutiny

---

## ARTICLE V — VALUE AND ASSET PRINCIPLES

### 5.1 Segregation
Donor funds must be held separately from platform operational funds at all times. Commingling is a constitutional violation.

### 5.2 Custody
No single individual may move donor funds unilaterally above defined thresholds. Multi-authorization is required for disbursements above the defined single-approver limit.

### 5.3 Transparency
Every dollar received, every fee taken, every disbursement made is recorded and publicly accessible in aggregate within 30 days of transaction.

### 5.4 Refundability
Donor refund paths exist and are documented. Donors who are deceived by fraudulent campaigns are made whole to the extent the platform has the ability to do so.

---

## ARTICLE VI — CHILD PROTECTION PRINCIPLES (NON-NEGOTIABLE)

These cannot be waived, overridden by a campaign operator, or traded for engagement metrics:

1. No identifiable child photo, name, or sub-city location is ever publicly displayed
2. Beneficiary data is collected only at the minimum level required for verification
3. Beneficiary data is never sold, shared with advertisers, or used for marketing
4. Campaign materials use dignified, age-appropriate representations only
5. Any third party with access to beneficiary data must sign a data protection agreement
6. Parent or guardian consent is required before any child data enters the system
7. Beneficiary records are purged on a defined schedule unless actively required for audit

---

## ARTICLE VII — FAILURE AND ACCOUNTABILITY

### 7.1 When the Platform Fails
If the platform cannot fulfill its mission (regulatory action, insolvency, catastrophic breach), there is a documented wind-down procedure that ensures:
- Donor funds in custody are returned or transferred to a designated successor organization
- Beneficiary data is securely destroyed
- Donors are notified
- Public disclosure is made

### 7.2 When a Campaign Fails
If a campaign is found to be fraudulent, inaccurate, or otherwise invalid:
- Funds not yet disbursed are frozen immediately
- Donors are notified
- Refunds are processed
- The campaign operator is suspended pending investigation
- Regulators are notified if legally required

### 7.3 Accountability Hierarchy
1. Child protection — overrides all other considerations
2. Donor fund integrity — overrides operational convenience
3. Legal compliance — overrides marketing or growth goals
4. Impact accuracy — overrides vanity metrics
5. Operational efficiency — subordinate to all of the above

---

## ARTICLE VIII — AMENDMENT

This constitution may be amended by the Platform Owner with 30-day public notice and documentation of what changed and why. Amendments that weaken child protection provisions require independent review before taking effect.
