## SECTION 1 — LEGAL ENTITY STATUS

### 1.1 — Required Legal Entity

**Current Status:** `[PENDING — COMPLETE BEFORE LAUNCH]`

The platform MUST operate under a properly formed legal entity. Options include:

| Entity Type | Characteristics | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **501(c)(3) Public Charity** | Tax-exempt, donations are tax-deductible, annual 990 required | Best for donor trust; longest to establish |
| **Fiscal Sponsorship** | Operate under an existing 501(c)(3) sponsor | Faster launch; share of revenues goes to sponsor |
| **LLC (Charitable Purpose)** | Not tax-exempt, donations NOT deductible; full flexibility | Acceptable for startup phase; less donor trust |
| **Benefit Corporation / B Corp** | Legally requires public benefit missions; not tax-exempt | Good mission signal; donations not deductible |

**Required determination:** Legal counsel selects appropriate structure and documents why.

## SECTION 6 — FUNDRAISING LEGAL REQUIREMENTS

### 6.1 — Required Disclosures (US — Varies by State)

Most states require specific disclosures in fundraising communications. A standard charitable solicitation disclosure will include:

```
[CHARITY NAME] is a [501(c)(3) / pending 501(c)(3)] organization.
EIN: [EIN]
Registered in [STATE] (registration #[NUMBER]).
A copy of our most recent financial report may be obtained by contacting us at [ADDRESS/EMAIL]
or from the [STATE] Attorney General's Charitable Trust Division.
```

**Legal counsel must confirm exact disclosure language for each solicitation state.**

### 6.2 — Fundraising Contract Requirements

Any paid fundraising activities (if ever used — not planned initially):
- Written fundraising contract required (state-specific)
- Percentage-of-donation fee arrangements disclosure required
- Not recommended for a platform-model charity
