# WHISTLEBLOWER POLICY
**Platform:** Child First Platform
**Version:** 1.0 | **Date:** 2026-04-02
**Owner:** Independent Director
**Hotline:** [To be created — see Section 3]

> This policy exists because any organization can be corrupted from within. Anyone — staff, volunteer, Campaign Operator, donor, or community member — who sees something wrong with how this platform handles children's welfare or donor funds must have a clear, protected path to report it. That path is not through the Platform Owner.

---

## SECTION 1 — PURPOSE AND SCOPE

### 1.1 — Purpose

This Whistleblower Policy establishes:
- What can be reported under this policy
- How to report safely
- What protection reporters receive
- How reports are investigated
- When external authorities are notified

### 1.2 — Scope

This policy applies to reports from any individual including:
- Platform employees and contractors
- Volunteers and advisors
- Campaign Operators and their staff
- Donors who have interacted with the platform
- Beneficiary organization staff or guardians
- Any member of the public with information about misconduct related to this platform

### 1.3 — What Can Be Reported

This policy covers reports of:
- Misuse or misappropriation of donated funds
- Fraud related to campaigns or impact claims
- Child data mishandling or privacy violations
- Child safeguarding failures (any harm or risk of harm to a child)
- Financial crimes (money laundering, falsification of records)
- Legal violations (COPPA, tax law, charitable solicitation law)
- Campaign Operator misconduct
- Platform staff misconduct
- Conflicts of interest affecting decision-making on the platform
- Cover-ups or obstruction of any of the above

---

## SECTION 2 — NON-RETALIATION COMMITMENT

**This platform does not retaliate against whistleblowers. Ever.**

Prohibited retaliation includes:
- Termination, suspension, or demotion of platform staff who report
- Removal of Campaign Operator status without documented, separate cause
- Harassment, intimidation, or threats
- Disparagement of the reporter's reputation
- Denial of benefits, access, or normal services to the reporter

If a person experiences retaliation after making a report, that retaliation is itself a separate reportable violation under this policy and is investigated by the same channel.

**The non-retaliation commitment is unconditional. It applies even if the initial report proves to be unfounded, so long as the report was made in good faith.**

A report made with knowing falsity to harm a specific person is not protected by this policy (that is not whistleblowing — it is defamation and will be treated accordingly).

## SECTION 3 — HOW TO REPORT

### 3.1 — Primary Reporting Channel

**All reports go to the Independent Director — NOT the Platform Owner.**

This separation is intentional. The most serious risks on this platform involve the Platform Owner's own conduct or the conduct of people the Platform Owner supervises. A reporting channel that goes through the Platform Owner is not independent.

**Independent Director contact:**
- Name: [Independent Director Name — to be appointed before launch]
- Private email: [Director email — not hosted on platform infrastructure]
- Secure reporting web form: [Third-party hosted anonymous form — e.g., EthicsPoint, Lighthouse, or similar]
- Postal address: [Mailing address — for reporters without internet access or requiring written trail]

### 3.2 — Anonymous Reporting

Reporters may report anonymously. Anonymous reports are accepted and investigated with the same seriousness as identified reports.

Anonymous reporters should use the secure reporting form or postal address. Phone or email reports can be traced to a degree and cannot be made fully anonymous by the reporter alone.

**Limitation:** If a report is anonymous and the investigation requires follow-up clarification, and the reporter cannot be reached, the investigation may be limited in scope. Reporters who can safely provide contact information are encouraged to do so with the expectation of confidentiality.

### 3.3 — Urgent Child Safety Reports

If a report involves **immediate risk of harm to a child**, do not wait for the reporting channel:
1. Contact local child protective services or law enforcement immediately
2. Contact NCMEC (National Center for Missing & Exploited Children): 1-800-843-5678
3. Then report to the Independent Director

The platform's internal reporting process is not a substitute for emergency child protection response.

## SECTION 4 — CONFIDENTIALITY

The Independent Director maintains strict confidentiality about:
- The identity of the reporter (if provided)
- The details of the report before conclusion of investigation
- The names of individuals being investigated

Confidentiality has limits:
- If sharing identity is required to investigate effectively, the reporter will be asked to consent before disclosure
- If law or law enforcement compels disclosure, the reporter will be notified to the extent permitted by law
- If the report involves an imminent threat to a child, confidentiality yields to protection

## SECTION 5 — WHAT HAPPENS AFTER A REPORT IS MADE

### 5.1 — Acknowledgment

A reporter who provides contact information receives:
- Acknowledgment of receipt within **48 hours**
- Reference number for their report
- Estimated timeline for initial assessment

Anonymous reporters receive no acknowledgment (by definition) but will see a published acknowledgment that the system is functioning if they check the platform's public accountability reporting.

### 5.2 — Initial Assessment (3 Business Days)

The Independent Director performs an initial assessment:
- Is the report within the scope of this policy?
- Does the report suggest an imminent threat (requiring emergency action)?
- What level of investigation is warranted?

Decision outcomes:
- **Out of scope:** Reporter notified; redirected to appropriate resource
- **In scope, non-urgent:** Formal investigation opened
- **In scope, urgent:** Emergency response triggered (see Section 6)
- **Requires law enforcement:** Immediately escalated (see Section 7)

### 5.3 — Formal Investigation

The Independent Director leads or appoints an investigator.

Timeline:
- **Preliminary findings:** Within 30 days of report receipt
- **Final report:** Within 60 days (or sooner if findings are clear)
- If the investigation requires more than 60 days, the reporter (if identified) is notified with a status update

Investigation principles:
- The subject of a report is presumed innocent during the investigation
- The subject is interviewed before a final conclusion is reached
- Evidence is gathered and preserved in accordance with applicable records laws
- No evidence is destroyed or suppressed during an open investigation

### 5.4 — Outcomes

Possible outcomes:
- **Substantiated:** Evidence supports the report. The Independent Director determines and implements or requires corrective action.
- **Unsubstantiated:** Investigation found no supporting evidence. File closed. No action against reporter.
- **Insufficient evidence:** Investigation unable to confirm or deny due to limitations. File noted; report may be reopened if new information emerges.

The reporter (if identified) receives written notification of the outcome to the extent that doing so does not violate the privacy of the investigated party.

---

## SECTION 6 — EMERGENCY RESPONSE

If at any stage an investigation reveals an active risk to donated funds or to children:

**Fund risk:**
- Independent Director has authority to instruct Finance Officer to freeze disbursements pending investigation
- This authority does not require Platform Owner approval
- Independent Director notifies Platform Owner in writing of the freeze and the reason

**Child risk:**
- Immediate referral to law enforcement and/or child protective services
- Campaign in question is immediately suspended from accepting donations
- Any pending disbursements to the organization in question are frozen
- Platform Owner notified

---

## SECTION 7 — EXTERNAL ESCALATION

The Independent Director escalates externally to:

| Authority | When |
|---|---|
| State Attorney General (charitable division) | Evidence of fund misuse or fraud that harms donors |
| IRS (Form 13909) | Evidence of 501(c)(3) tax exemption abuse |
| FinCEN | Evidence of money laundering or AML violations |
| NCMEC CyberTipline | Any child sexual exploitation material |
| Local law enforcement | Any imminent physical threat to a child or other person |
| FBI | Cybercrime, large-scale fraud, threats crossing state lines |

External escalation does not require Platform Owner consent. It is the Independent Director's independent obligation.

---

## SECTION 8 — ANNUAL ACCOUNTABILITY REPORT

Each year, the platform publishes a public summary of:
- Number of reports received (without identifying details)
- Nature of reports (categories only)
- Number investigated; outcomes (substantiated / unsubstantiated / other)
- Any corrective actions taken
- Any external escalations made

This report demonstrates that the reporting channel is functioning and that the non-retaliation commitment is real.

---

## SECTION 9 — POLICY AVAILABILITY

This policy must be:
- Published in full on the platform's public website
- Referenced in every Campaign Operator agreement
- Included in the onboarding information for all platform volunteers and staff
- Included in the donor-facing FAQ and legal pages

The reporting contact details must be visible without requiring a login.

---

## SECTION 10 — INDEPENDENT DIRECTOR INDEPENDENCE REQUIREMENTS

The person serving as Independent Director under this policy must:
- Have no financial dependency on the Platform Owner (no employment, no business relationship)
- Have no family relationship with the Platform Owner
- Be appointed by a board decision or equivalent governance process, not unilaterally by the Platform Owner
- Have the explicit authority, documented in the platform's governance documents, to freeze funds and escalate externally without Platform Owner consent

**[PRE-LAUNCH BLOCKER: An Independent Director meeting these requirements must be identified and appointed before this policy is active and before the platform accepts any donations.]**
