# OPERATOR MODEL
**Platform:** Child First Platform
**Version:** 1.1 | **Date:** 2026-04-02 (enhanced — training curriculum expanded)

---

## PURPOSE

This document defines who operates this platform, what their responsibilities are, required qualifications, succession planning, and accountability structures. A platform for children's welfare cannot depend on heroic individuals or informal arrangements.

---

## SECTION 1 — OPERATIONAL ROLES

### 1.1 — Platform Owner

**Description:** The founding operator and ultimate responsible party for all platform operations, legal compliance, and governance.

**Responsibilities:**
- Final authority on all platform policy decisions
- Co-signatory on all fund disbursements (no exceptions)
- Responsible for maintaining legal entity standing, tax status, and regulatory compliance
- Final escalation point for all operational incidents
- Ensures succession plan is documented and current
- Responsible for Independent Director relationship

**Minimum Requirements:**
- Individual (not a company role) — specific named person accountable
- Legal capacity to enter contracts in the operating jurisdiction
- Not currently subject to any legal proceedings related to financial fraud, child welfare violations, or fiduciary misconduct

**Accountability:**
- Accountable to: Independent Director, legal entity board (if applicable), and donors via public transparency reports
- Cannot override Independent Director on emergency resolution
- Cannot unilaterally change the platform's governance documents

---

### 1.2 — Finance Officer

**Description:** Manages day-to-day financial operations including donation tracking, disbursement initiation, account reconciliation, and audit preparation.

**Responsibilities:**
- Daily reconciliation of all platform financial accounts
- Initiating (not approving) disbursement requests
- Maintaining accurate donation and disbursement records
- Preparing monthly internal audit reports
- Flagging any financial anomalies to Platform Owner and Independent Director
- Maintaining compliance with applicable financial regulations

**Minimum Requirements:**
- Background check (financial industry-standard depth)
- No history of financial crime or fiduciary misconduct
- Financial or accounting background (CPA, bookkeeper, or equivalent experience)
- Not a family member of the Platform Owner (conflict of interest)

**Accountability:**
- Reports to: Platform Owner
- Independent oversight: Campaign Auditor reviews all disbursement documentation
- Cannot approve own disbursement requests
- Financial actions are audit-logged and reviewed quarterly by Independent Director

---

### 1.3 — Child Privacy Officer (CPO)

**Description:** The designated authority responsible for all policies, procedures, and enforcement relating to child beneficiary data. The CPO's privacy decisions override all other operational decisions.

**Responsibilities:**
- Reviewing and approving all beneficiary record submissions
- Enforcing the data minimization and retention policies
- Annual privacy audit of all systems touching beneficiary data
- Reviewing partner organization consent processes
- Sole authority to access or delete beneficiary records
- Responding to beneficiary data breach incidents (P0 incident lead)
- Training campaign operators and staff on child data policies

**Minimum Requirements:**
- Background check (enhanced — child-protection relevant)
- Experience with data privacy law (COPPA, GDPR, or equivalent)
- No conflict of interest with any Campaign Operator on the platform
- Independent of Platform financing (not compensated based on platform revenue)

**Succession:**
- CPO role cannot be vacant. Before any planned departure, a successor must be confirmed and trained.
- If CPO leaves without succession: all beneficiary data operations halted until replacement confirmed.
- Succession candidate must be pre-designated in writing and updated annually.

---

### 1.4 — Campaign Auditor

**Description:** Independent reviewer of campaign milestone documentation and disbursement requests. The primary control preventing milestone disbursements without evidence.

**Responsibilities:**
- Reviewing all disbursement requests against submitted milestone documentation
- Independently verifying that documentation supports the milestone claimed
- Approving or rejecting disbursement requests (not initiating)
- Escalating questionable documentation to Platform Owner
- Maintaining independence from Campaign Operators (no financial relationship)

**Minimum Requirements:**
- Not employed by or financially related to any Campaign Operator on the platform
- Background check
- Familiarity with NGO/charitable program delivery documentation standards

**Independence Requirement:**
- Campaign Auditor cannot hold equity in or receive compensation from Campaign Operators
- Must disclose any personal or professional relationship with Campaign Operators before reviewing their campaigns
- Conflicts of interest must be documented; auditor must recuse from reviewing related campaigns

---

### 1.5 — Independent Director

**Description:** An external governance authority who provides oversight on emergency actions, high-value fund movements, and governance disputes. The Independent Director has no operational role — only governance authority.

**Responsibilities:**
- Co-signing all disbursements above the $10,000 threshold
- Co-authorizing Emergency Freeze and Emergency Freeze lift
- Annual review of financial audits
- Reviewing and resolving disputes escalated past the Platform Owner
- Holding the succession activation authority if Platform Owner is unavailable

**Minimum Requirements:**
- Not employed by or financially dependent on the platform
- Not a family member of the Platform Owner or Finance Officer
- Legal, financial, nonprofit governance, or child-welfare background preferred
- Named individual (not an institutional role)
- Agreement documented and current

---

### 1.6 — Campaign Operators

**Description:** Organizations or individuals who create and manage fundraising campaigns on the platform. They are not platform staff — they are accountable third parties operating under a binding agreement.

**Onboarding Requirements:**
- [ ] Legal entity type and registration documented
- [ ] Individual identity verification (government-issued ID) for the authorized representative
- [ ] Business/organization verification (registration documents)
- [ ] Signed Campaign Operator Agreement (reviewed by platform legal counsel)
- [ ] Bank account documentation for any disbursement destinations
- [ ] Acknowledgment of child protection policies
- [ ] Acknowledgment of the milestone-based disbursement model
- [ ] Acknowledgment that fraudulent claims constitute legal breach

**Ongoing Obligations:**
- Annual re-verification
- Immediate notification of any material changes to the organization (leadership change, legal issues, address change)
- Full cooperation with audit requests

**Suspension and Termination:**
- Operators may be suspended pending investigation
- Operators may be terminated and banned for: fraud, false impact claims, child data violations, non-cooperation with audits
- Termination does not release legal obligations under the operator agreement

---

### 1.7 — Verifiers

**Description:** Independent parties who confirm that impact claims made by Campaign Operators are supported by real-world evidence.

**Types:**
- **Field Verifiers:** Visit beneficiary sites to confirm aid delivery
- **Document Verifiers:** Review submitted documentation (receipts, manifests, third-party reports)
- **Third-Party Organization Verifiers:** NGOs or government bodies who provide independent attestation

**Independence Requirement:**
- Verifiers may not verify campaigns with which they have a financial, organizational, or personal relationship
- Conflicts declared in advance; recusal required
- A campaign may not be marked "Verified" without at least one verifier with no discernible affiliation to the Campaign Operator

## SECTION 4 — COMPETENCY AND TRAINING REQUIREMENTS

### 4.1 — Mandatory Training by Role

Training must be completed before account activation and renewed annually. Failure to renew disables account access after a 30-day grace period.

#### All Internal Roles (Platform Owner, Finance Officer, CPO, Campaign Auditor, Independent Director)

| Training Module | Format | Renewal | Completion Requirement |
|---|---|---|---|
| Child Protection Fundamentals | Self-paced course + written assessment | Annual | Pass assessment with ≥ 80% score |
| COPPA and GDPR Awareness | Self-paced course | Annual | Completion certification |
| Child Safeguarding and Recognizing Harm | Accredited external course (see note) | Annual | Certificate from approved provider |
| Platform Security Awareness (phishing, social engineering, account security) | Self-paced course | Annual | Pass assessment |
| Anti-Money Laundering (AML) Awareness | Self-paced course | Annual | Pass assessment |
| Recognizing and Reporting Campaign Fraud | Self-paced course | Annual | Completion certification |
| Non-Retaliation Policy (Whistleblower) | 1-page attestation | Annual | Signed attestation on file |

**Child safeguarding note:** Approved providers include the Child Welfare Information Gateway training resources, NCMEC training programs, and accredited third-party platforms (e.g., Safeguarding Alliance, Virtual College Level 2 Child Protection). The CPO is responsible for approving the provider list.

### 4.2 — Training Records

- All training completions are logged in an internal training registry with: role, name, training name, completion date, expiry date, and evidence (certificate or assessment result)
- The Platform Owner is responsible for maintaining the registry
- The Independent Director reviews the training registry annually
- Access is revoked if training lapses. Reactivation requires training completion — not a grace extension

### 4.3 — New Role Onboarding Timeline

Before any new person starts exercising an internal platform role:

| Step | Timing |
|---|---|
| Background check initiated | Before any access is provisioned |
| Background check clear | Before any authorization is granted |
| All role-specific training completed | Before account activation |
| Access provisioned per CONTROL_AND_PERMISSIONS_MAP.md | After training confirmed |
| First 90 days: supervised operation | Finance Officer, CPO, Campaign Auditor only |
| First 90-day review completed | 90 days after start |
