# DONOR RIGHTS AND DISCLOSURES
**Platform:** Child First Platform
**Version:** 1.0 | **Date:** 2026-04-02
**Standard:** Genesis Elite Humanitarian & Fund-Me Build Standard v2.0

> Donors are partners in this mission. They extend trust to us in the act of giving. That trust must be protected — legally, ethically, and technically.

---

## SECTION 1 — PRE-DONATION DISCLOSURES (REQUIRED BEFORE TRANSACTION)

The following information MUST be visible to a donor BEFORE they submit payment. They may not be buried in terms of service, a separate page, or fine print below the donation button.

### 1.1 — Platform Fee Disclosure

**Required format (example):**
> "A platform fee of [X]% is deducted from your donation to cover operating costs. If you donate $100, [$100 - fee] goes to this campaign. You may choose to add the fee on top so 100% of your stated amount reaches the campaign."

- Fee percentage must be accurate and up-to-date in real time
- If the fee is zero, that must be stated clearly (and the funding source of operations disclosed)
- Donor must be given the option to cover the fee (not required, not default-checked)

### 1.2 — Campaign Status Disclosure

Before donating, donors must be shown:
- Whether the campaign has completed identity verification ("Verified Operator" badge or warning if not)
- Whether the campaign's impact claims have been independently verified ("Verified Impact" badge or "Claims Unverified")
- The current funding level vs. goal
- The campaign close date (or that it is open-ended)
- The name of the operating organization behind the campaign

### 1.3 — Fund Usage Disclosure

**Required statement before donation:**
> "Funds raised by this campaign are held in trust and disbursed by [Platform Name] only after milestone verification. [Platform Name] does not provide upfront funding to campaign operators before results are confirmed."

### 1.4 — Tax-Deductibility Disclosure

**If tax-deductible:**
> "Donations to this campaign may be tax-deductible as a charitable contribution to [Legal Entity Name], a [501(c)(3)/applicable designation]. A receipt will be provided."

**If NOT tax-deductible (or status is pending):**
> "This platform does not currently offer tax-deductible donation receipts. [OR: Tax-deductibility is pending legal review. Do not treat this donation as a charitable deduction without consulting a tax advisor.]"

**PROHIBITED:** claiming or implying tax-deductibility without confirmed legal basis.

## SECTION 2 — DONOR RIGHTS

Every donor has the following rights, regardless of donation size:

### Right 1 — Right to Know Where the Money Goes
Donors may request a detailed breakdown of how their donation was used, including platform fee deducted, amount disbursed to campaign, and milestone it was disbursed against. Response must be provided within 10 business days.

### Right 2 — Right to a Receipt
A donation receipt must be sent within 24 hours of payment confirmation. The receipt must include:
- Donation amount
- Date
- Platform fee (if any)
- Net amount to campaign
- Transaction reference number
- Donor name and email
- Campaign name
- (If applicable) tax-deductibility statement

### Right 3 — Right to Refund
Donors may request a full refund within 30 calendar days of their donation if:
- The campaign has not yet received a disbursement against that donation
- The refund request is submitted via the documented refund channel
Refund processing time: maximum 10 business days.

### Right 4 — Right to See Impact Reports
Donors may view the published impact reports for any campaign they have donated to, once reports are available. Platform must not restrict impact report access to paid tiers.

### Right 5 — Right to Request Campaign Pause
If a donor believes a campaign is fraudulent or misleading, they have the right to submit a formal dispute. The platform must:
- Acknowledge the dispute within 24 hours
- Provide an investigation update within 7 days
- Resolve or escalate within 30 days

### Right 6 — Right to Deletion of Personal Data
Donors may request deletion of their personal data, subject to legally required retention periods (e.g., financial records that must be retained for tax purposes). Non-required data must be deleted within 30 days of request.

### Right 7 — Right to Contact a Human
There must always be a human-staffed support contact (email minimum; chat preferred) available for donor questions. Automated-only support is insufficient.

### Right 8 — Right Not to Be Marketed To Without Consent
Donor email, phone number, and demographic data may not be used for marketing unless the donor has explicitly opted in. This opt-in must be separate from the donation flow — it is not implied by donating.

## SECTION 3 — PROHIBITED DONOR-FACING PRACTICES

The following are explicitly prohibited:

| Practice | Classification |
|---|---|
| Pre-checking the "cover the fee" option (default opt-in) | Dark pattern — PROHIBITED |
| Claiming tax-deductibility without legal basis | Fraud — PROHIBITED |
| Showing inflated donation totals (e.g., including platform matching promises not yet confirmed) | Misrepresentation — PROHIBITED |
| Making the refund process materially harder than the donation process | Dark pattern — PROHIBITED |
| Implying urgency ("Only $50 left to save this child!") with fabricated or unverified numbers | Manipulation — PROHIBITED |
| Sending unsolicited marketing to donors who did not opt in | Legal violation — PROHIBITED |
| Sharing donor identity with campaign operators without donor consent | Privacy violation — PROHIBITED |
| Hiding the platform fee in checkout totals | Deceptive practice — PROHIBITED |

## SECTION 4 — DISPUTE RESOLUTION PROCESS

### 4.1 — Donor Dispute Flow

```
Donor submits dispute (via support channel)
    └── Platform acknowledges within 24 hours with ticket number
         └── Finance Officer reviews within 7 days
              └── If fund has not been disbursed: refund initiated
                   └── If fund has been disbursed: escalated to Campaign Auditor
                        └── Campaign Auditor reviews disbursement documentation
                             └── Decision within 30 days total
                                  └── If fraud confirmed: donor refund from Reserve Account
                                       └── If no fraud found: documented explanation to donor
```

### 4.2 — Escalation Path

If a donor is unsatisfied with the resolution:
- Platform Owner review (7 additional days)
- Independent Director review (if Platform Owner review is disputed)
- External: donors retain the right to seek regulatory remedy (state charity bureau, BBB, etc.)

## SECTION 5 — RECURRING DONATION RULES

If the platform offers recurring donations:

1. Recurring donation frequency and amount must be clearly displayed before the donor commits
2. Recurring donor must receive a reminder email 5 days before each charge after the first
3. Cancellation must be possible with ONE action — not buried in an account menu, not requiring a phone call
4. Cancellation takes effect before the next scheduled charge (not after)
5. If a campaign closes while a recurring donation is active: donor must be notified and asked to redirect or cancel — funds may not be automatically transferred to another campaign

---

## SECTION 6 — ANONYMOUS DONATION POLICY

Anonymous donations are permitted. However:
- The platform must still collect transaction data for financial and legal purposes
- "Anonymous" means the donor name is not displayed publicly
- The platform retains the donor's identity internally for fraud, refund, and legal purposes
- Anonymous donations above a documented threshold require additional verification to comply with AML requirements
